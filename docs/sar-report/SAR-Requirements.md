# Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) Requirements
## Accountancy Sector Template Guide

This document outlines the complete requirements for creating and publishing a Suspicious Activity Report (SAR) for the accountancy sector, based on the UK template.

---

## Money Laundering Definition

**Money laundering** describes any activity involving dealing in, using, possessing, retaining, converting, disguising, or otherwise benefitting from proceeds of crime. It includes involvement in arrangements facilitating such dealings, use, retention, possession, or other benefit.

**Proceeds of crime** include any monies or assets deriving directly or indirectly from criminal conduct, including:
- Tax evasion
- Theft
- Fraud
- Corruption

---

## Section 1: Introduction

### 1.1 Introduction
**Purpose:** Explain in simple terms how and where the suspicion has arisen.

**Requirements:**
- Provide a short summary to help law enforcement understand the context
- Outline the suspicion and circumstances in which it arose
- **State whether the report relates to suspected money laundering OR terrorist financing activities**
- Indicate what other information may be available

**Example:**
> "The following [money laundering/terrorist financing] suspicion has arisen in the course of providing professional services [describe the generic service] to [name of client/recipient of the service] who is a [describe their activities and general location]."

---

### 1.2 Professional Services Being Provided
**Purpose:** Describe the nature of professional services and their relevance to the suspicion.

**Requirements:**
- **Start with "PROFESSIONAL SERVICES"** to identify the context
- Provide brief explanation of services being provided where relevant to the suspicion
- Use codes for accountancy-specific services where possible (see Appendix)

**Examples of services:**
- Statutory audit of financial statements / personal tax compliance / administration of a pension scheme
- Financial due diligence / transactional advisory services

---

### 1.3 Client/Recipient of Service
**Purpose:** Identify Main Subject / Associated Subjects relevant to the suspicion.

**Important Notes:**
- s337(1) POCA protects reporters from potential breaches of confidentiality
- Only include information directly connected to the suspicion being reported
- **Restrict Associated Subjects to those directly connected with suspected ML/TF activity**
- Give details of other parties and their involvement (victim, suspect, unknown) in Section 2

**For Individuals, include (where known):**
- Full name(s)
- Date(s) of birth
- Nationality
- Identification number(s)
- Full address(es) including postcode(s)
- Telephone numbers

**For Entities, include (where known):**
- Legal name
- Any trading name(s)
- Country of incorporation
- Registered addresses
- Trading/business addresses
- Details of beneficial owners (if relevant to the suspicion)

**Additional Requirements:**
- Indicate where addresses are current, previous, last known, business, residential, etc.
- **State where information is not known** (e.g., "address UNKNOWN")

---

### 1.4 Activities/Location
**Purpose:** Describe the activities and location of the subject.

**May include:**
- Job title
- Companies House reference
- Email or IP address
- Summary from directory/website

**Example:**
> "E.g. meetings with the management / audit committee / finance director, reviewing documents relating to [...]"

---

### 1.5 Situation in Which Knowledge/Suspicion Arose
**Purpose:** Describe the specific situation that led to the suspicion.

**Requirements:**
- Describe what has been heard or observed
- Explain how the criminal activity has taken or will take place

---

### 1.6 Previous Report Reference
**Purpose:** Reference any previously submitted reports to assist law enforcement.

**Requirements:**
- Give the date [month/year] and [URN]
- **Use the UK URN for the previous SAR, NOT your own reference number**

**Example:**
> "The suspicion was initially reported in [date and URN]. Further detail has now been obtained as a result of [describe additional updates]."

---

### 1.7 Reason for Additional Update
**Purpose:** Explain why you are providing an update to a previous report.

**Example:**
> "Information received from the client / further review of information in the course of the engagement / discussions with third parties"

---

## Section 2: Suspicion or Knowledge

### 2.1 Describe What Has Been Heard or Observed
**Purpose:** Explain the context, introduce Associated Subject(s), and describe the discovered criminality.

**Requirements:**
- Explain what the proceeds of crime consist of
- Quantify them if possible
- Explain how the criminal activity has taken or will take place

**Example:**
> "In the course of providing professional services [describe the nature of the professional services] the reporter [describe what has been heard or observed]."

---

### 2.2 Nature of Criminal Property and Quantum
**Purpose:** Describe the suspected criminal/terrorist property.

**Requirements:**
- Provide a **chronological sequence of events** where possible
- **Avoid jargon, acronyms, and abbreviations**
- Give details of any documentation or sources that may be relevant
- **State if the value or quantum is UNKNOWN**
- An estimate is still useful if clearly stated as such
- Give as much information as possible regarding location, address of suspected property

**Example:**
> "The suspected proceeds of crime are [describe the type and quantum - e.g. property worth £x, cash totalling £x, other movable stolen property, unpaid tax]."

---

### 2.3 Reason for the Suspicion
**Purpose:** Answer the key questions about the suspicious activity.

**Required Information (5 W's):**
- **Who** is involved?
- **How** are they involved?
- **Where** is the criminal/terrorist property?
- **When** and how did your knowledge/suspicion arise?
- **Why** do you know or suspect the property is criminal/terrorist property?

---

### 2.4 Glossary Code
**Purpose:** Help the NCA quickly assign and cross-reference the information.

**Requirements:**
- Insert the relevant glossary code at the beginning of this section
- **State "NO CODE APPLIES" if no glossary codes are available**

---

### 2.5 Other Parties
**Purpose:** Identify any other party/parties involved.

**Requirements:**
- Include addresses and dates of birth if known
- **State if not known**

---

### 2.6 Other Relevant Information
**Purpose:** Include any other information relevant to the knowledge/suspicion that can be shared.

**Important:**
- **Do not include attachments**

---

## Section 3: Reason for Disclosure

### 3.1 Link to Criminal Activity
**Purpose:** Connect the knowledge/suspicion to the ML/TF activity by identifying the criminal activity, linking it to criminal proceeds, and describing the ML/TF offence.

**Requirements:**
- Identify who is known/suspected to have engaged in criminal activity
- Refer to the Main Subject, Associated Subject, etc.

---

### 3.2 Criminal Activity
**Purpose:** Describe the criminal conduct as you understand it.

**Requirements:**
- Describe the conduct - what do you think is happening?
- What is the conduct that has raised concerns?
- If you know the specific legislation (e.g., Bribery Act, Theft Act, Fraud Act), you may refer to it
- Describe the criminal conduct using the language in legislation (e.g., "fraud by misrepresentation")

**Example:**
> "The Main Subject is suspected of [describe the criminal activity], such activity being considered an offence under [name of legislation, if known].
>
> As a result of this criminal activity, it is suspected that the [Main Subject] may have [ML/TF offence] [SAR Code XXXXXX] the proceeds of crime and as a result may have committed money laundering / terrorist financing offences under POCA 2002 / TA 2000."

---

### 3.3 Money Laundering / Terrorist Financing Offence
**Purpose:** Identify the specific ML/TF offence.

**Money Laundering:**
- Concealing
- Acquiring
- Use
- Possession
- Becoming concerned in an arrangement

**Terrorist Financing:**
- Fundraising
- Use or possession
- Arranging
- Concealing
- etc.

---

### 3.4 SAR Code for ML/TF Activity
**Purpose:** Use the relevant code to categorize the activity.

**Examples:**
- **XXD5XX** - Concealing
- **XXD6XX** - Transferring

---

## Section 4: Other Information

### 4.1 Reports to Other Authorities
**Purpose:** State whether suspicions have already been reported to relevant authorities or whether further reports may follow.

**Requirements:**
- Name of law enforcement body
- Include relevant reference number if known

**Possible Authorities:**
- Metropolitan Police
- HMRC
- SFO
- FCA
- Interpol

**Example:**
> "It is believed that the matter has already been reported to [name of relevant law enforcement or government body] on [include date and year if known].
>
> It was reported that the [relevant individual] has [action taken].
>
> It is understood that there are proceedings underway involving [parties] to [purpose of proceedings]."

---

### 4.2 Relevant Individuals
**Purpose:** Name any known parties to any relevant proceedings.

**Requirements:**
- Refer to Main Subject / Associated Subject, etc.
- Name any known parties to relevant proceedings

---

### 4.3 What Has Happened?
**Purpose:** Describe actions taken or proceedings underway.

**Examples:**
- Dismissed by their employer
- Contract terminated
- Ongoing SFO investigation

---

## Section 5: Defence Against Money Laundering (XXS99XX)

**Purpose:** Seek a defence to carry out a prohibited act (e.g., to continue with a matter that you know or suspect involves ML).

**Important Note:**
- **A defence cannot be granted simply to continue acting for a client**

---

### 5.1 Prohibited Act
**Purpose:** Specify what defence is required and under which section of POCA.

**Requirements:**
- What is the prohibited act (i.e., the specific ML offence) you are seeking a defence for?
- Specify the section in POCA (sections 327-329):
  - **Section 327** - Dealing with criminal proceeds
  - **Section 328** - Arranging or facilitating the use, control, acquisition, or retention of proceeds
  - **Section 329** - Acquisition, use, or possession of proceeds

**Example:**
> "This SAR seeks a defence to a money laundering / terrorist financing offence in relation to [describe transaction] under section 327*/328*/329* POCA 2002. Set out the amount or value of the suspected criminal proceeds or the best estimate of this amount or value [indicate whether the amount is an estimate]. * delete as appropriate"

---

### 5.2 Relevant Individuals
**Purpose:** Identify the individual(s) you know or suspect to be involved in ML.

**Requirements:**
- Give details of the vendor/purchaser in a sale/purchase
- Give the amount of any consideration / transaction value

---

### 5.3 Rationale
**Purpose:** Explain the basis for the knowledge/suspicion.

**Requirements:**
- What are the events/activities that led you to become suspicious?
- The report may be relied upon to make the decision to complete a transaction
- The liquidator will make payments to creditors or take possession of criminal property

---

### 5.4 Criminal Property Description
**Purpose:** Enable the NCA to consider and assess the application.

**Requirements:**
- Include a description of the criminal property
- Its whereabouts (e.g., bank details)
- Its amount or value

---

### 5.5 Next Steps
**Purpose:** Describe the next steps to be taken in the matter/transaction.

**Requirements:**
- Provide a timetable of steps you need to take

---

## Section 6: Contact Details

### 6.1 MLRO Name
**Purpose:** Provide the name of the Money Laundering Reporting Officer.

**Requirements:**
- Name of MLRO (the NCA maintains a list)
- This can be the Deputy MLRO if necessary

**Example:**
> "If you require any further information, please contact our MLRO [name of MLRO] on [contact details]."

---

### 6.2 Contact Information
**Purpose:** Provide complete contact information for follow-up.

**Requirements:**
- Work or mobile numbers
- Email addresses

## Key Guidelines for SAR Completion

### General Principles
1. **Be Clear and Concise** - Use simple terms to explain suspicions
2. **Avoid Jargon** - Do not use acronyms or abbreviations without explanation
3. **Be Chronological** - Present events in order where possible
4. **State Unknowns** - Always indicate when information is not known
5. **No Attachments** - Do not include attachments with the SAR
6. **Estimates Are Useful** - Provide estimates when exact figures are unknown, but clearly mark them as estimates

### Critical Requirements
- **Always state** whether the report relates to money laundering OR terrorist financing
- **Always use "PROFESSIONAL SERVICES"** at the start of Section 1.2
- **Always protect confidentiality** under s337(1) POCA
- **Always use UK URN** (not your own reference number) when referencing previous SARs
- **Always state "NO CODE APPLIES"** if no glossary codes are available
- **Always specify POCA sections** (327-329) when seeking a defence

### SAR Codes Reference

**Money Laundering Activities:**
- **XXD5XX** - Concealing
- **XXD6XX** - Transferring

**Defence Against Money Laundering:**
- **XXS99XX** - Seeking defence to carry out prohibited act

## Submission Process

1. **Complete all relevant sections** of the SAR template
2. **Review for completeness** - ensure all required fields are filled
3. **Verify codes** - check that all SAR codes and glossary codes are correct
4. **Submit to NCA** - through the appropriate reporting channel
5. **Retain copy** - keep records in accordance with regulatory requirements
6. **Follow up if required** - respond to any NCA requests for additional information

---

## Legal Framework

**Primary Legislation:**
- **POCA 2002** (Proceeds of Crime Act 2002) - Money laundering offences
- **TA 2000** (Terrorism Act 2000) - Terrorist financing offences

**Key Sections:**
- **Section 327 POCA** - Concealing, disguising, converting, transferring, or removing criminal property
- **Section 328 POCA** - Entering into or becoming concerned in an arrangement to facilitate acquisition, retention, use, or control of criminal property
- **Section 329 POCA** - Acquiring, using, or possessing criminal property
- **Section 337(1) POCA** - Protection from breach of confidentiality for reporters
